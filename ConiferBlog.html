Conifer Lovers

Conifers are a group of trees in the division Pinophyta. They are gymnosperms, meaning that they produce seeds. And they are known for reproducing through seeds found in cones, not in fruits or through spores like other trees. There are six types of Conifers:

  • Pine
  • Hemlock
  • Larch
  • Fir
  • Spruce
  • Ginkgo

Pine

Pine trees have sharp needles that are generally bunched in bundles.

Hemlock

Hemlocks have cones that point downwards.

Larch

Larch are deciduous trees that lose their leaves in the Fall, and have needles that bunch together.

Fir

Fir trees have cones that face upwards.

Spruce

Spruce trees have needles that are attached directly to the branch and are alone.

Ginkgo

Lastly, Ginkgo trees have heart-shaped leaves instead of needles. They are the last of their kind.
